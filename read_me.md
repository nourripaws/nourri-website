## PROJECT OVERVIEW

### Concept
NOURRI (from French "nourrir" = to nourish) produces functional wellness cookies for dogs. Each variety targets specific health needs using fresh, natural ingredients.

### Positioning
- **NOT luxury** (like TakiPets)
- **Functional wellness** at accessible premium prices
- **Dehydrated** (not freeze-dried) for broader market appeal
- **Long-lasting chews** (5-15 minutes vs 30 seconds)
- **Botanical decoration** (edible flowers/herbs)

### Target Market
- **Primary (70%):** Health-conscious pet owners, budget-aware
- **Secondary (30%):** Premium-curious (can't afford freeze-dried regularly)
- **Total addressable:** 80% of Singapore pet treat market

### Unique Selling Points
1. Four functional varieties (targeted health benefits)
2. Long-lasting texture (dental benefits, value)
3. Beautiful natural colors (Instagram-worthy)
4. Accessible premium pricing (S$16-26 vs S$28-40 freeze-dried)
5. Made fresh in Singapore
6. Grain-free, single-protein base

## RECIPES (METRIC)

### Universal Production Method (All Recipes)
1. Prep fresh ingredients (cook/blend as needed)
2. Mince chicken to paste in food processor
3. Whisk eggs + coconut oil + any liquid ingredients
4. Add chicken paste + dry ingredients + functional ingredients
5. Mix until uniform color (thick paste consistency)
6. Fill silicone molds (~20g dough per cavity)
7. Press botanical decoration 2-3mm into surface
8. **FREEZE 30-60 minutes** (critical for mold release!)
9. Pop out chilled domes, arrange on dehydrator trays
10. Dehydrate at 63-65°C (rotate trays halfway through)
11. **POST-HEAT: 135°C oven for 10 minutes** (kills bacteria - MANDATORY!)
12. Cool completely (2-3 hours minimum)
13. Package with oxygen absorber immediately

### Universal Base (Recipes 1, 2, 3)
- **Chicken breast:** 500g (minced to paste)
- **Eggs:** 3 large eggs (~150g total)
- **Coconut oil:** 20ml (reduced from 30ml for better fat ratio)
- **Ground flaxseed:** 20-30g (adjust for moisture)

### Recipe 1: Joint Support (Golden) 🟠

**Base Ingredients:**
- Chicken breast: 500g
- Eggs: 150g (3 large)
- Coconut oil: 20ml
- Ground flaxseed: 20g

**Functional Ingredients:**
- Turmeric powder: 20g
- Ground ginger: 5g
- Unflavored gelatin powder: 20g
- Cold water (for gelatin): 45ml

**Alternative to Gelatin:**
- Chicken skin/cartilage (minced): 100g
- (If using: reduce chicken to 400g)

**Decoration:**
- Calendula OR chamomile flowers: 10-15 flowers

**Gelatin Prep:**
1. Pour 20g gelatin into bowl
2. Add 45ml cold water, stir
3. Let sit 5 minutes (becomes gel)
4. Microwave 10 seconds until liquid
5. Mix into egg mixture before adding chicken

**Dehydration:** 63-65°C for 10-12 hours  
**Post-heat:** 135°C for 10 minutes  
**Yield:** ~50 cookies (~170g dried) = 3-4 packages

**COGS:** S$3.10 + S$0.28 electricity + S$0.50 packaging = **S$3.88 per package**  
**Retail:** S$20-22  
**Margin:** 82-84%

### Recipe Notes & Critical Reminders

**Temperature & Time:**
- Dehydration: 63-65°C only (NOT higher - preserves nutrients, colors)
- Post-heat: 135°C for 10 minutes (MANDATORY - kills bacteria)
- Total time: 12-14 hours + 10 min + 2-3 hours cooling

**Food Safety:**
- 63-65°C dehydration does NOT kill bacteria (just removes moisture)
- 135°C post-heat is what makes treats SAFE
- Never skip post-heat treatment
- Cool completely before packaging (prevents condensation/mold)

**Mold Release:**
- MUST freeze 30-60 minutes before releasing from molds
- This is CRITICAL for clean release
- Light oil spray on molds helps (optional)

**Doneness Test:**
- Break cookie in half
- Must be bone dry inside (no soft/moist center)
- Should snap cleanly, not bend

**Storage:**
- Package immediately after cooling (airtight)
- Use oxygen absorber (especially salmon recipe)
- Label with batch code (YYMMDD-###) and best before date
- Room temp: 6-8 weeks (salmon: 3-4 weeks)
- Refrigerated: 3-4 months

**Coconut Oil Adjustment:**
- Reduced to 20ml for Recipes 1-3 (better fat ratio 22-23%)
- Keep 30ml for Recipe 4 (omega fats support skin/coat benefits)

**Chamomile Source:**
- MUST be 100% pure chamomile tea bags
- Check ingredients: Should say "chamomile flowers" ONLY
- No added sugar, honey, essential oils, or other herbs
- Safe brands: Twinings Pure Chamomile, Dilmah Chamomile
- Available at FairPrice (~S$4-5)

## EQUIPMENT & PRODUCTION

### Equipment Owned
**PowerPac PPT512 Dehydrator:**
- Cost: S$92.90 (purchased)
- Power: 245 watts (0.245 kW)
- Temperature range: 35-70°C
- Capacity: 5-6 trays
- Yield: ~50 cookies per batch

### Equipment Needed
**Silicone Molds:**
- **Recommended:** 15-cavity hemisphere molds
- **Size:** 3.6cm (1.4") diameter x 2cm (0.8") height
- **After dehydration:** Shrinks to ~1.1-1.2" diameter
- **Portion:** ~20g raw dough = ~6g dried per cookie
- **Quantity needed:** 2-3 molds (S$16-30 total on Shopee/Lazada)

**Kitchen Scale:**
- Digital, 0-2kg capacity
- 1g precision
- Essential for metric measurements
- ~S$15-25

**Food Processor or Blender:**
- For mincing chicken to paste
- Blending spinach
- Any basic model works

**Other Tools:**
- Mixing bowls (various sizes)
- Whisk
- Spatula
- Measuring cups (ml)
- Oven (for post-heat treatment at 135°C)

### Production Process (Per Batch)

**Time Breakdown:**
- Prep ingredients: 30-45 minutes
- Mix dough: 15 minutes
- Fill molds: 20-30 minutes
- Freeze: 30-60 minutes
- Dehydrate: 10-14 hours (overnight)
- Post-heat: 10 minutes
- Cool: 2-3 hours
- Package: 15-20 minutes

**Total active time:** ~2-2.5 hours  
**Total elapsed time:** ~16-20 hours (mostly passive)

**Optimal Schedule:**
- **Evening (7pm):** Prep, mix, fill molds, freeze
- **Evening (8:30pm):** Start dehydrator, go to sleep
- **Morning (9-10am):** Post-heat treatment
- **Afternoon (12-1pm):** Package and label

**Batch Frequency:**
- Part-time: 2 batches/week (8 batches/month)
- Moderate: 4 batches/week (16 batches/month)
- Full production: 1 batch/day (30 batches/month)

## KEY DECISIONS MADE

### 1. Production Method: Dehydration (Not Freeze-Drying)
**Why:**
- Lower equipment cost (S$93 vs S$3,000+)
- Faster production (10-14 hours vs 24-48 hours)
- Better texture for most dogs (long-lasting chew)
- Larger target market (70-80% vs 20-30%)
- Part-time friendly (run overnight)
- Lower electricity cost (S$0.87 vs S$9-18 per batch)

**When to add freeze-dryer:**
- Month 7-12 after launch
- When profitable
- Launch "NOURRI RAW" premium line
- Dual product lines serve full market

### 2. Recipe Format: Fresh Ingredients (Not Powders)
**Why:**
- More accessible (available at FairPrice/wet markets)
- Lower cost (no specialty powder markup)
- Better marketing story ("fresh spinach, not powder")
- Vibrant natural colors (no artificial dyes needed)
- Easier to source in Singapore

**Savings:** S$13-24 per batch vs using specialty powders

### 3. Coconut Oil Reduction: 20ml (Not 30ml)
**Why:**
- Lower fat content (22-23% vs 26%)
- Healthier for dogs
- Still binds well with eggs + flaxseed
- Cost savings: S$0.30-0.40 per batch

**Exception:** Keep 30ml for Skin & Coat recipe (omega fats support the theme)

### 4. Company Structure: Incorporate Solo First
**Why:**
- Get UEN number immediately (needed for licenses)
- Control during critical setup phase
- Add Founder B within 3 months while valuation low
- Easier than waiting for partner commitment

**Timeline:**
- March 2026: Incorporate solo
- April-May 2026: Add Founder B (transfer 4,000 shares @ S$1)
- May 2026: Launch together

### 5. Website Platform: GitHub + Vercel (Not Shopify)
**Why:**
- FREE vs S$39/month (S$468/year savings!)
- Professional setup
- Learn valuable skills
- Can migrate to Shopify later if needed
- Perfect for pre-launch landing page

**When to consider Shopify:**
- Doing 50+ orders/month
- Need automated inventory management
- Want all-in-one solution
- Can afford S$39/month

### 6. Domain Choice: .com (Not .sg)
**Recommendation:** Buy nourri.com first
**Why:**
- Cheaper (S$13-18 vs S$38-45)
- Global reach (can sell beyond Singapore later)
- More professional
- Can add .sg later when business grows

### 7. Post-Heat Treatment: 135°C for 10 Minutes (MANDATORY)
**Why:**
- 63-65°C dehydration does NOT kill bacteria
- 135°C kills ALL pathogens (Salmonella, E. coli, Listeria)
- Singapore SFA compliance
- Legal liability protection
- Customer safety

**Never skip this step!** Even at 75°C dehydration, post-heat still required.

## IMPORTANT REMINDERS

### Food Safety (Non-Negotiable)
1. ✅ Post-heat treatment at 135°C for 10 minutes ALWAYS
2. ✅ Cool completely (2-3 hours) before packaging
3. ✅ Use oxygen absorbers (especially salmon)
4. ✅ Label with batch code and best-before date
5. ✅ Store in airtight containers immediately
6. ✅ Maintain production logs (date, batch, ingredients)

### Quality Control
1. ✅ Break cookie in half - must be bone dry inside
2. ✅ Should snap cleanly, not bend
3. ✅ No soft/moist center
4. ✅ Colors should be vibrant (not brown/dull)
5. ✅ Botanical decoration intact

### Business Compliance
1. ✅ File annual returns with ACRA (company secretary handles)
2. ✅ File corporate tax return (hire accountant S$800-1,500/year)
3. ✅ Maintain proper bookkeeping (invoices, receipts, bank statements)
4. ✅ Keep SFA license current
5. ✅ Maintain product liability insurance

### Customer Service

3. ✅ Provide tracking numbers
4. ✅ Ask for feedback after delivery
5. ✅ Handle complaints professionally
6. ✅ Build email list for announcements
